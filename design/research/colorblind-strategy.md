# Wave 3 — Vision modes strategy (HC + 4 color blindness types)

**Date**: 2026-04-26
**Branch**: `design-tokens`
**Goal**: add 5 accessibility modes to the openTRNTBL DS — 1 high-contrast + 4 color blindness modes (deuteranopia / protanopia / tritanopia / achromatopsia). Approach A: 1 mode per type, all V1, achromatopsia included.

---

## 1. Inventory of existing core ramps

Source: `design/tokens/src/core.tokens.json` (generated by `generate-core.py`).

Scale: 11 shades per hue (`0, 10, 25, 50, 75, 100, 125, 150, 175, 190, 200`) — Fusilier-style convention. Luminance is uniform at fixed shade across all hues (binary-search OKLCH L → Y target). Chroma follows a bell curve centered on 100, floor = 15% of peak at the extremes to keep a visible hue at the edges.

| Hue | OKLCH H° | Chroma peak | L range | Shades | Hex at 100 |
|---|---:|---:|---|---:|---|
| `neutral` | 0° | 0.00 (pure gray) | 0 → 1 | 11 | `#757575` |
| `red` | 29° | 0.18 | 0 → 1 | 11 | `#d14437` |
| `orange` | 55° | 0.17 | 0 → 1 | 11 | `#b75e00` |
| `gold` | 85° | 0.13 | 0 → 1 | 11 | `#957000` |
| `yellow` | 100° | 0.17 | 0 → 1 | 11 | `#a79300` |
| `green` | 145° | 0.17 | 0 → 1 | 11 | `#3ea748` |
| `cyan` | 195° | 0.13 | 0 → 1 | 11 | `#00a4a4` |
| `blue` | 255° | 0.19 | 0 → 1 | 11 | `#0075e2` |
| `violet` | 300° | 0.18 | 0 → 1 | 11 | `#8e5cd4` |

**Total: 9 hues × 11 shades = 99 core.palette.\* tokens** already available.

Hues mapped to primitive roles:
- `accent` → `gold`
- `success` → `green`
- `warning` → `orange`
- `danger` → `red`
- `info` → `blue`
- `neutral` → `neutral`

The `cyan`, `yellow`, `violet` hues already exist at the core level but **are aliased by no primitive** today. That's what we'll exploit for the color blindness modes.

## 2. Inventory of existing modes

On the **tokens side (semantic.tokens.json)**:
- `color:dark` — implemented on `surface`, `border`, `accent`, `text.color.*`, `status.{success,warning,danger,info}.{bg,text}`.
- `density:compact` / `density:spacious` — implemented on `spacing.{tight,snug,default,loose,airy}`.

**No `vision:*` or `contrast:*` mode exists in semantic.tokens.json today.** The spec mentioned "already-existing empty modes" — they are actually in **Storybook (`.storybook/preview.js`)** declared empty on the toolbar side:

```js
contrast: { items: [{value:'default'},{value:'enhanced'}] },
vision:   { items: [{value:'default'},{value:'protan'},{value:'deutan'},{value:'tritan'}] },
```

The toolbar applies `data-contrast` and `data-vision` on `<body>`, but no `[data-contrast="enhanced"]` or `[data-vision="..."]` CSS selector exists in `tokens.css` → switching does nothing today. That's what we'll fix in B.

**Missing on the Storybook side**: `achromatopsia` is not in the vision list (it'll need to be added in B.4).

**The `generate-css.py` pipeline is mode-agnostic**: every `axis:value` found in `com.opntrntbl.modes` transforms into `[data-{axis}="{value}"]`. So we have no pipeline change to make to add `vision:*` and `contrast:enhanced` — just fill in the modes in semantic.tokens.json.

## 3. Proposed status × vision-mode mappings

### Reference sources

- **Wong palette** (Bang Wong, *Nature Methods*, 2011, "Points of view: Color blindness") — 8 colors distinguishable by all forms of color blindness. Reference hex: `#000000`, `#E69F00`, `#56B4E9`, `#009E73`, `#F0E442`, `#0072B2`, `#D55E00`, `#CC79A7`. Source: <https://www.nature.com/articles/nmeth.1618>.
- **IBM Design Color-Blind-Safe Palette** — Wong variant calibrated for IBM Carbon data-viz.
- **Tableau Color Blind 10** — equivalent palette, focus on business dataviz. Source: Stone, Albers Szafir, Setlur (2014), "An Engineering Model for Color Difference as a Function of Size", Tableau Research.
- **Coblis simulators** (Color Blindness Simulator) — confusions per type validated on these palettes: <https://www.color-blindness.com/coblis-color-blindness-simulator/>.

### Mapping principles

1. **AAA target maintained**: we stay on the pair `bg=hue.190 / text=hue.50` for light, `bg=hue.10 / text=hue.175` for dark. This pair gives 8.83-8.91 (light) / 13.52-13.61 (dark) on all hues — confirmed by `wcag_check.py`.
2. **No more saturated chroma than what we have**: our OKLCH-calibrated ramps are already at max sRGB gamut. Distinguishability comes from **hue difference**, not absolute saturation.
3. **Mandatory icons** on all vision modes (Alert and StatusBadge) — no color signal without form reinforcement. Verified: Alert already has icons per variant (info/warning/error/success). To note for Wave 4: **StatusBadge has no icon per variant today**, only a dot — a distinctive shape will need to be added in achromatopsia mode (cf. flag §7).

### Mapping per mode

#### `vision-deuteranopia` (green missing — ~6% of men, the most frequent)

Deutan confusions: red ↔ green ↔ brown, and secondarily pink ↔ gray. Blue and yellow remain stable.

| Status | Source hue | Justification |
|---|---|---|
| `success` | **cyan** (195°) | replaces `green` (deutans confuse it with red). Cyan distinct from red/orange because dominant blue component. |
| `warning` | **yellow** (100°) | replaces `orange` which becomes ambiguous with red in deutan. Pure yellow remains distinguished. |
| `danger` | **orange** (55°) | replaces `red` (deutans would see red and orange identical in danger/warning so we choose one of the two; orange has more luminance). |
| `info` | `blue` (255°) | unchanged, the blue-yellow axis remains readable. |

Wong reference: success ≈ `#56B4E9` (sky blue), warning ≈ `#F0E442` (yellow), danger ≈ `#D55E00` (vermillion), info ≈ `#0072B2` (blue).

#### `vision-protanopia` (red missing — ~2% of men)

Similar to deutan but red also loses luminance (appears darker/brown). Confusions: red ↔ black, red ↔ green, orange ↔ green.

| Status | Source hue | Justification |
|---|---|---|
| `success` | **blue** (255°) | green confused with red/brown for protans. Blue is the safe cool axis. |
| `warning` | **yellow** (100°) | unchanged from deutan; yellow remains readable. |
| `danger` | **violet** (300°) | orange may seem brown/dark for protans → switch to magenta/violet (blue component distinct from yellow warning). |
| `info` | **cyan** (195°) | differentiate from blue of success. |

Tableau CB10 reference: danger in magenta/pink (#CC79A7), success in blue.

#### `vision-tritanopia` (blue missing — ~0.01%, very rare)

Confusions: blue ↔ yellow, blue ↔ gray, violet ↔ red. Red/green remain **stable** for tritans.

| Status | Source hue | Justification |
|---|---|---|
| `success` | `green` (145°) | unchanged, the red-green axis is intact for tritans. |
| `warning` | `orange` (55°) | unchanged, distinct from red/violet in luminance. |
| `danger` | `red` (29°) | unchanged. |
| `info` | **violet** (300°) | replaces `blue` (confused with yellow/gray). Violet has a red component that distinguishes it. |

#### `vision-achromatopsia` (monochrome — very rare ~0.003%)

Distinguishes **only luminance**. No color signal at all. The strategy relies on:
1. **Bg luminance differentiation**: choose 4 distinct gray levels for the 4 statuses.
2. **Mandatory icons**: the shape carries the semantics (warning = triangle, danger = octagon, etc.).
3. **Strict AAA abandoned in favor of AA + distinguishability**: on 11 neutral shades, finding 4 pairs simultaneously AAA and luminance-separated forces compromises. AA (>= 4.5) with icons is more accessible than AAA + invisible icons.

| Status | Light bg / text | Dark bg / text | Ratio (light/dark) |
|---|---|---|---|
| `success` | `n.190` / `n.25` | `n.0` / `n.175` | 13.34 / 14.73 — AAA |
| `warning` | `n.175` / `n.25` | `n.25` / `n.190` | 10.48 / 13.34 — AAA |
| `danger` | `n.100` / `n.200` | `n.100` / `n.0` | 4.61 / 4.56 — AA (mid-grey, ink inverted for "alarm" effect) |
| `info` | `n.150` / `n.0` | `n.50` / `n.200` | 9.68 / 10.05 — AAA |

The `danger` bg at `n.100` (mid-grey) is intentional: it is the **most intense** visually (the most saturated in luminance-difference from the canvas), which marks the urgency.

## 5. HC plan (high-contrast)

### Specification

- **Mode key**: `contrast:enhanced` (consistent with the existing Storybook declaration: `contrast: { items: ['default','enhanced'] }`).
- **Light HC**:
  - `surface.canvas` → `neutral.200` (#fff pure)
  - `surface.base` → `neutral.200`
  - `text.color.primary` → `neutral.0` (#000 pure)
  - `text.color.secondary` → `neutral.10` (instead of `n.75`, to remain ink-dense)
  - `border.subtle` → `neutral.100` (was `n.175`)
  - `border.default` → `neutral.50` (was `n.150`)
  - `border.strong` → `neutral.0` (was `n.100`)
  - `border.focus` → `accent.50` (was `accent.100`) — even more inky
  - Status bg → tints `190`, status text → shade `25` (instead of `50`) for 13+ ratio (was 8.8)
- **Dark HC**:
  - `surface.canvas` → `neutral.0` (#000 pure)
  - `text.color.primary` → `neutral.200` (#fff pure)
  - `border.strong` → `neutral.200`
  - Status bg → `10`, status text → `190` (instead of `175`) for 17+ ratio
- **Focus ring**: framed in the mode-extension of `border.focus`, already calibrated for AAA.

### Mode combinations

`contrast:enhanced` is **stackable with `color:dark`**. The `generate-css.py` pipeline generates a `[data-contrast="enhanced"]` selector that applies in addition to `[data-color="dark"]`. For specific overrides **dark + enhanced**, we use the DTCG composite key convention: `color:dark|contrast:enhanced` (mentioned in `generate-semantic.py` doc).

**V1 Decision**: we **don't** use composite combinations. `contrast:enhanced` reacts to the current values of the color mode via natural CSS cascade (selectors are cumulative). If an override has to change between HC-light and HC-dark, we just encode two distinct values in `contrast:enhanced` mode → not necessary, we push the **most contrasted average values** that work in both color modes (e.g., `n.0` text works in light HC, `n.200` text works in dark HC). Cascade: `[data-color="dark"][data-contrast="enhanced"]` re-overrides dark-native values with their contrast-enhanced version.

Concretely, for each token concerned we'll have:
```json
"com.opntrntbl.modes": {
  "color:dark":         "<dark default>",
  "contrast:enhanced":  "<HC light value — text=neutral.0>",
  "color:dark|contrast:enhanced": "<HC dark value — text=neutral.200>"
}
```

The `generate-css.py` pipeline must handle the composite key. Code audit (`_collect` line 218): it does `axis, val = mode_key.split(':', 1)` — so `'color:dark|contrast:enhanced'` won't match. **Limitation**: we'll have to **extend `generate-css.py`** to handle the `|` separator, OR settle for `contrast:enhanced` overrides without dark variant.

### Pragmatic V1 strategy

To avoid touching `generate-css.py` (risk, scope creep), we limit HC to **single values per token**:
- For tokens where the enhanced value is the same in light and dark (e.g., `border.strong` → `n.0` light HC, `n.200` dark HC: different), we have to accept a compromise OR extend the pipeline.

**Decision**: we **extend `generate-css.py`** to support `axis1:val1|axis2:val2` → emit `[data-axis1="val1"][data-axis2="val2"]`. Small clean extension, ~10 lines. This extension serves not only HC but also any future mode crossing.

**To flag for Ismail**: is this pipeline extension acceptable in B.2? If not, fallback = HC only covers tokens whose HC value is identical light/dark (e.g., status colors if we push them to common saturated extremes). Automatic detection impossible without the extended pipeline.

## 6. WCAG validation

Script: `design/tokens/scripts/wcag_check.py`. Runs the calculation of all ratios for the 5 mappings + HC.

### Results — bg/text pairs (target AAA = 7.0)

| Mode | Light min/max | Dark min/max | Verdict |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| `default` | 8.83 / 8.91 | 13.52 / 13.61 | ✅ AAA |
| `vision-deuteranopia` | 8.83 / 8.91 | 13.52 / 13.61 | ✅ AAA |
| `vision-protanopia` | 8.83 / 8.91 | 13.52 / 13.61 | ✅ AAA |
| `vision-tritanopia` | 8.83 / 8.91 | 13.52 / 13.61 | ✅ AAA |
| `vision-achromatopsia` | 4.61 / 13.34 | 4.56 / 14.73 | ⚠️ AA min (danger), AAA for 3/4 |
| `contrast:enhanced` (HC) | 13.20 / 13.30 | 17.21 / 17.31 | ✅ AAA strict |
| HC neutrals (text/canvas) | 21.00 | 21.00 | ✅ MAX |

### Pairwise distinguishability — detected limits

The 4 color blindness modes rely on **different hues** but at the same shades (190 light bg, 10 dark bg). So the **luminance** between the bg is quasi-identical (ratio ~1.00) → distinguishability comes from **chrominance** (hue difference), not luminance.

**Implication**: the mapping is valid for the 4 hue-based color blindness types, because these users **distinguish** hues. Pairwise luminance is just a robustness test for the degenerate case (achromatopsia), for which we have a dedicated mode (#vision-achromatopsia).

For **achromatopsia specifically**, distinguishability is:
- light: success/danger 4.12 ✅, success/info 1.94 ✅, warning/danger 3.23 ✅, warning/info 1.52 ⚠️ (>1.5 min), success/warning 1.27 ❌ (but icons remove the ambiguity), danger/info 2.12 ✅
- dark: similar pattern

→ **icons mandatory in achromatopsia**: without them, success vs warning would be too close in pure luminance.

## 7. Decisions to flag for Wave 4 (visual) review

No blocking ambiguity for B. But to flag for the visual review:

1. **Violet vs magenta for danger in protan**: our `violet` (300°) is more "berry/eggplant" than "magenta/alarm". If the Wave 4 review finds that an "alarm" feel is missing, add a dedicated `magenta` hue (~330°). Not blocking V1.
2. **Yellow saturation**: our `yellow` at 100° and chroma_peak 0.17 reaches `#a79300` at shade 100 (rather olive-green). Verify visually that it's still perceived as "warning" and not as "mustard green" (for deutan, it must clearly contrast with cyan success).
3. **StatusBadge without icon in achromatopsia**: today StatusBadge only has a pulsing dot. In achromatopsia/vision-* mode, the dot isn't enough. **Wave 4 recommendation**: add an SVG icon per variant (or a distinctive shape — dot/triangle/square/cross).
4. **Cyan luminance**: our `cyan` at 195° is very "blue-cyan" (close to turquoise). For deutan, it must contrast with `blue` info — verify they don't get confused (a priori not, axis H = 195° vs 255° = 60° difference, sufficient).
5. **HC dark text/border pushed values**: pushing to pure `neutral.200` may seem too harsh visually (sharp edge). Evaluate whether we keep `n.190` (#f2f2f2) which is the "near-light" anti-banding or we accept the pure `n.200` in HC (HC mode must be maximal, so pure recommended).
6. **Pipeline `generate-css.py` `|` composite extension**: technical decision — accept the PR or do a fallback. Cf. §5.
